Sand Comparison

A view of the different types of sand available in the US that are deemed to be safe for corydoras catfish.

CaribSea Super Naturals

This sand is extremely fine. It's practically a powder and when on the hand, it 'sticks'. There are no sharp edges and it does not feel grainy. It's an off-white color.

QuikCrete Play Sand

Also a very soft sand, very fine, sort of powdery. This one is very close in feel to the CaribSea, but is not as 'sticky' on the hand.

QuikCrete Pool Filter Sand

A very natural looking sand with larger, varied grain size. Not sticky, nor sharp.

Black Diamond Blasting Sand, Fine, 20/40

Very soft. Glittery. Very dusty. In the images below you can see it looks sharp. It's not. No damage to my hands, not even any pain when grinding it between my fingers. Because I'm crazy, I took a fingertip full and rubbed it on the inside of my lip. No pain, no cuts or even a raw feeling. This is the item purchased: Amazon

Aqueon Shrimp and Plant Substrate

Feels very smooth. I found 1 that had a bit of the black coating coming off of it, but it was not easily peeled off and I could not crush the bead by hand. Very hard, but light weight. Kind of like 'buckshot'.
